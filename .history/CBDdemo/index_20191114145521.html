Cold-pressed hemp seed oil

Hemp seed oil comes from the small seeds of the Cannabis sativa plant. Vitamins and fatty acids from hemp seed oil may be especially helpful for the skin, brain, and heart. It has been shown to treat eczema, psoriasis, and atherosclerosis, and has even been shown to work as a natural pain reliever.

Sunflower oil

Sunflower oil is the non-volatile oil pressed from the seeds of the sunflower. It is used as a source of unsaturated fat in the diet to replace saturated fats. Sunflower oil is most commonly used to treat high cholesterol and preventing heart disease.

MCT Oil (purified coconut oil)

Medium-chain triglyceride (MCT) oil contains medium-length chains of fats called triglycerides. Due to their shorter length, MCTs are easily digested compared to other sources of fats. MCT oil is most commonly extracted from coconut oil, as more than 50% of the fat in coconut oil comes from MCTs. MCT oil has been shown to promote weight loss, increase energy, and reduce lactic acid buildup.

Lecithin

Lecithin is a fat that is essential in the cells of the body. It can be found in many foods, including soybeans and egg yolks. Lecithin is used for treating memory disorders such as dementia and Alzheimer's disease. It works in the body by converting to acetylcholine, a substance that transmits nerve impulses. Lecithin is also used for treating gallbladder disease, liver disease, certain types of depression, high cholesterol, anxiety, and eczema.

Copaiba

Copaiba is a stimulant oil-resin obtained from the trunk of several types of pinnate-leaved South American leguminous trees. The people who live in areas where copaiba trees grow have long used copaiba oil-resin for a variety of different purposes. Some of these include use as an anti-inflammatory, promotion of wound healing, and use for pain relief.
